About LeaderRate

Why the project exists and how it was put together.

LeaderRate was shaped as a simple public-interest platform for FOSCO: a place where people can speak honestly under codenames, track visible updates, and keep office feedback grounded in real experience.

The idea

How it came about

The project started from a practical gap: people often have concerns, suggestions, and praise about campus offices, but many do not have a safe and structured way to share them openly. LeaderRate was designed to make that process simpler, more respectful, and easier to follow over time.

The build

How it was created

The product is built as a web application using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Supabase. That approach keeps the interface fast, the deployment simple, and the data model flexible enough for profiles, feedback, updates, and review workflows.

Creator

Who created it

LeaderRate was created by Theophilus Baffoe as a focused software project around anonymous participation, public accountability, and clearer institutional communication.

Team

Who manages it

The project is managed by the LeaderRate team and collaborators who maintain the product direction, content standards, operational setup, and future improvements around the platform.

What it aims to do

What success looks like

The goal is not noise. The goal is useful signal: clearer feedback, visible follow-through, and a healthier loop between people who raise issues and the offices expected to respond to them.
